Is it actually worth $795? Find yourself below.

The annual fee is $795. Whether the card pays for itself depends entirely on which credits you'd genuinely use. Here are three honest scenarios — not the biggest number we could print.

Persona 1 — Maximum theoretical

~$2,916/year → +$2,121 net after the $795 fee

Here's the honest catch: this only works if you already want Apple Music and Apple TV+ and a Peloton membership and DashPass and Lyft rides every month and DoorDash every month — and you fully use the hotel credits on top. Almost nobody captures all of it. We show this number for completeness, not as a promise.

Persona 2 — Realistic frequent traveler

~$960–$1,210/year → +$165 to +$415 net

A realistic basket: $300 annual travel credit + $300 Exclusive Tables dining credit + $300 StubHub credit + $60 DoorDash restaurant credit = $960 ($165 above the fee). Add one 2-night stay through The Edit (+$250) and you're at $1,210 (+$415). For a genuine frequent traveler, the card pays for itself — modestly, but it pays.

Persona 3 — Casual user

~$360/year → −$435 net

A casual basket: $300 travel credit + $60 DoorDash restaurant = $360, which is $435 less than the fee. If you're closer to this persona, the card likely costs more than it returns, and a no-annual-fee travel card may serve you better. The card is built for heavy travelers, and the math says so.

We didn't round any of these up. The credits, dollar values, and conditions all come straight from the verified table below.

Five things the affiliate sites won't emphasize

  1. 1. The 2027 cliff.

    Several headline credits are time-boxed: Apple Music and Apple TV+ through June 2027, Lyft through September 2027, Peloton and StubHub through December 2027. Chase may renew them, modify them, or let them lapse. Don't buy the card today assuming these are permanent — buy it for the credits that aren't on a timer. The $300 annual travel credit, the $300 Exclusive Tables dining credit, and the protection suite have no stated expiration.

  2. 2. The Edit hotel credit has traps.

    The $500 Edit credit starts January 1, 2026, caps at $250 per transaction, and requires a two-night minimum stay. Book a single-night trip and you forfeit half of it. It's real value, but only if your travel happens to fit Chase's shape.

  3. 3. The DoorDash maze resets monthly.

    There's roughly $420 of DoorDash-adjacent value — $60 restaurant credit, $240 in non-restaurant promos, and a $120 DashPass subscription — but it all resets every month. An unused month is gone forever. This is only worth real money if you already order DoorDash regularly.

  4. 4. Global Entry is not an annual credit.

    The application-fee credit is up to $120 once every four years — about $30 a year when you spread it out. We count it as $30, not $120, and you should too when you do your own math.

  5. 5. The best perks are locked behind $75,000 of spending.

    The Southwest credit, the Shops at Chase credit, and the airline/hotel elite statuses all require $75,000 in annual spend. For most cardholders that's out of reach, so we keep this tier out of the headline value rather than pad the number.

What we left out of the table — and why

  • Insurance benefits. The card carries an extensive protection suite (auto rental, trip cancellation, baggage, evacuation, purchase and return protection). We exclude these from the value math because they only pay out when something goes wrong — real protection, but not money you can count on every year.
  • Lounge access. Priority Pass, Chase Sapphire Lounges, and partner lounges have genuine value, but it varies so much by how often you fly that any single dollar figure would be misleading. We'd rather say nothing than invent a number.
  • Points and earn rates. This page is about statement credits, not rewards earning. Points Boost and category multipliers deserve their own honest analysis, which we're not going to fake here.
  • The spend-gated tier. The $75,000-spend benefits are only relevant to a small subset of cardholders, so we keep them out of the headline value rather than let them inflate it.

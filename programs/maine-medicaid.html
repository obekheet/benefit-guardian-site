MaineCare (Maine Medicaid)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

MaineCare is Maine Medicaid program. It provides free and low-cost health insurance and health benefits to eligible Maine residents, including children, parents, older adults, and people with disabilities. MaineCare is administered by the Maine Department of Health and Human Services.

What is MaineCare?

MaineCare is the name for Medicaid in Maine. It offers comprehensive health coverage including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, dental services, and long-term care for those who qualify. Coverage and costs vary depending on your category of eligibility and income.

Who may be eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and sometimes disability or age. Children, pregnant individuals, parents and caretakers, adults under expanded coverage, older adults, and people with disabilities may qualify. Income limits vary by group, so it is worth applying even if you are unsure.

How to apply for MaineCare

You can apply online through My Maine Connection, by mail, by phone, or in person at a DHHS office. The Office for Family Independence processes MaineCare applications and determines eligibility. You may be asked to provide proof of income, residency, and household details.

Official source: Maine DHHS - MaineCare (Medicaid)

Frequently asked questions

Is MaineCare the same as Medicaid?

Yes. MaineCare is the name Maine uses for its Medicaid program. It provides free and low-cost health coverage to eligible residents.

What does MaineCare cover?

MaineCare covers a broad range of services including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, dental, and long-term care, depending on your eligibility category.

How do I apply for MaineCare?

Apply online at My Maine Connection, by mail, by phone, or in person at a DHHS office. The Office for Family Independence handles applications.

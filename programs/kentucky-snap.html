Kentucky SNAP (Food Assistance)

Last updated: June 2026

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) in Kentucky helps people with low income buy food. In Kentucky, the program is administered by Kentucky DCBS. This guide explains who qualifies, the maximum monthly benefit amounts, and how to apply.

What is SNAP in Kentucky?

SNAP provides monthly benefits on an EBT card that can be used to buy groceries at most stores and many farmers markets. Benefit amounts depend on household size, income, and certain expenses.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based mainly on household income and size. Most households must have a gross monthly income at or below 130% of the federal poverty level. Work requirements may apply to some adults. Check with Kentucky DCBS for the rules that apply to your situation.

How to apply

  • Online: Kentucky DCBS online application
  • By phone: 1-855-306-8959
  • In person / by mail: Contact your local Kentucky DCBS office.

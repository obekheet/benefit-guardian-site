Government Benefit Programs You May Qualify For

Billions of dollars in government benefits go unclaimed every year simply because people don't know they're eligible. These plain-English guides explain who qualifies for major federal programs, how much they're worth, and how to apply — with links to official government sources.

SNAP (Food Stamps)

Monthly help buying groceries — up to ~$973/mo for a family of four.

Learn more →

WIC

Healthy food and nutrition support for women, infants, and children under 5.

Learn more →

Medicaid

Free or low-cost health coverage for eligible low-income people.

Learn more →

Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

Refundable tax credit worth up to $7,830 for working families.

Learn more →

Child Tax Credit (CTC)

Up to $2,000 per child under 17, partly refundable.

Learn more →

SSI

Monthly cash for people 65+, blind, or disabled with limited income.

Learn more →

LIHEAP

Help paying home heating and cooling bills and energy emergencies.

Learn more →

CHIP (Kids' Health)

Low-cost health coverage for children in families that earn too much for Medicaid.

Learn more →

ACA Marketplace Subsidies

Premium tax credits that lower the cost of Marketplace health insurance.

Learn more →

Social Security Retirement

Monthly retirement income based on your work history — up to $4,018/mo in 2025.

Learn more →

NJ ANCHOR Property Tax Relief

New Jersey property tax rebate — up to $1,750 for homeowners and $700 for renters.

Learn more →

NJ FamilyCare (Medicaid/CHIP)

New Jersey’s Medicaid and CHIP program — free or low-cost health coverage for eligible residents.

Learn more →

CalEITC (California EITC)

California’s refundable Earned Income Tax Credit — up to $3,756 for low-income working families and individuals.

Learn more →

CalFresh (California SNAP)

California’s food assistance program — monthly food benefits on an EBT card for low-income households.

Learn more →

Medi-Cal (California Medicaid)

California’s Medicaid program — free or low-cost health coverage for eligible low-income residents.

Learn more →

CARE/FERA Utility Discounts

California energy bill discounts — CARE gives 30–35% off electric and 20% off gas; FERA gives 18% off electric.

Learn more →

Florida SNAP (Food Assistance)

Monthly food assistance on an EBT card for eligible low-income Florida households.

Learn more →

Florida Homestead Exemption

Reduce your home’s taxable value by up to $50,000 on your Florida property taxes.

Learn more →

Florida Medicaid

Free or low-cost health coverage for eligible low-income Florida residents.

Learn more →

Florida KidCare

Income-based health and dental insurance for Florida children; many pay $15–$20/month.

Learn more →

Georgia TANF (Cash Assistance)

Last updated: June 13, 2026

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) provides temporary cash assistance and support services to help low-income Georgia families with children meet their basic needs while working toward self-sufficiency.

What is Georgia TANF?

TANF offers time-limited monthly cash payments to eligible families with dependent children. Beyond cash assistance, the program emphasizes employment and self-sufficiency, often connecting participants to work, training, and supportive services. It is administered by the Department of Human Services, Division of Family & Children Services (DFCS).

Who is eligible?

TANF generally serves low-income families with children (and pregnant individuals) who meet income, resource, and other program requirements. Eligibility is based on household size, income, and circumstances, and is determined by your county DFCS office. Work participation requirements typically apply to adult recipients.

How to apply

You can apply online through the Georgia Gateway member portal, in person at your county DFCS office, or by mail. After applying, you may need to complete an interview and provide documentation of income and household composition.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does TANF provide?

TANF provides temporary monthly cash assistance and support services to eligible low-income families with children.

Is TANF time-limited?

Yes. TANF is designed as temporary assistance, with time limits and work participation requirements aimed at helping families move toward self-sufficiency.

Where do I apply for Georgia TANF?

Through the Georgia Gateway online portal or your local county DFCS office.

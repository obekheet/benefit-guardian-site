Families First (Tennessee TANF)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Families First is the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) program in Tennessee, administered by the Tennessee Department of Human Services (TDHS). It provides temporary cash assistance and support services to eligible families with children.

What is Families First?

Families First is the cash assistance portion of the Tennessee TANF program. It provides temporary monthly cash payments along with employment, training, and support services aimed at helping families become self-sufficient. TDHS reviews applications and determines eligibility.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household income, resources, and the presence of dependent children, and recipients generally agree to a personal responsibility plan with work or training activities. Pregnant women and families with minor children may qualify. Applying is the best way to learn what you can receive.

How to apply

You can apply for Families First through the One DHS Customer Portal online, by mail, or in person at a local TDHS office. After applying, you may need to complete an interview and provide documentation of income, household, and other details.

Official source

For official, up-to-date information, visit TDHS Families First (tn.gov).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Families First the same as TANF?

Yes. Families First is the name of the Tennessee Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) program.

Who administers Families First?

The Tennessee Department of Human Services (TDHS) administers the Families First program.

Where do I apply for Families First?

You can apply through the One DHS Customer Portal online, by mail, or in person at a local TDHS office.

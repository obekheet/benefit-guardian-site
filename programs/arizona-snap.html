Arizona SNAP (Nutrition Assistance)

Last updated: June 19, 2026

In Arizona, the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) is called Nutrition Assistance (NA). It helps eligible low-income Arizona households buy groceries each month. The program is administered by the Arizona Department of Economic Security (DES), Family Assistance Administration, and benefits are delivered on an EBT card.

What is Nutrition Assistance in Arizona?

Nutrition Assistance provides monthly food benefits loaded onto an EBT card, which works like a debit card at any store that accepts SNAP — and for approved online grocery orders. Benefits can be used for foods like fruits and vegetables, meat, poultry and fish, dairy, and bread and cereal. They cannot be used for tobacco, alcohol, vitamins, medicines, or non-food household items.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources. Working families, older adults, people with disabilities, and others with limited income may qualify. Arizona DES screens every application for expedited service, which can provide benefits within seven days for households with very little income or resources. Most adults ages 16 to 59 must meet general SNAP work requirements unless they qualify for an exemption.

How to apply

You can apply for Arizona Nutrition Assistance in several ways. After applying, you will complete an interview and provide documents to verify income and household details.

Apply online

Create an account and apply through Health-e-Arizona Plus at healthearizonaplus.gov, where you can also check your case and upload documents.

Apply by phone

Call 1-855-432-7587 (1-855-HEA-PLUS) for help applying and to request free language assistance.

Apply by mail or in person

You can also submit an application at a local DES Family Assistance Administration office.

Official source

This guide is based on information from the official program sources: Arizona DES — Food Assistance and the USDA FNS SNAP eligibility standards. Income limits and benefit amounts can change; always confirm current figures with Arizona DES.

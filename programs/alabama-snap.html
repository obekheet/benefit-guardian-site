Alabama SNAP / Food Assistance

Last updated: June 14, 2026

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) helps eligible low-income Alabama households buy groceries each month. SNAP is administered by the Alabama Department of Human Resources (DHR).

What is SNAP in Alabama?

SNAP provides monthly benefits on an EBT card that can be used to buy food at most grocery stores in Alabama. The exact benefit amount varies based on household size, income, and certain expenses.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources. Working families, older adults, people with disabilities, and others with limited income may qualify. Alabama DHR reviews each application against current income limits.

How to apply

You can apply for SNAP online through MyDHR, by mail, or in person at a local county DHR office. After applying you will complete an interview and provide documents to verify income and household details.

Official source

This guide is based on information from the official program source: Alabama DHR — Food Assistance (SNAP).

Frequently asked questions

How much will I get from SNAP in Alabama?

The amount varies based on your household size, income, and allowable expenses. Alabama DHR calculates your benefit when you apply.

Where can I use my SNAP benefits?

SNAP benefits are loaded onto an EBT card and can be used to buy eligible food items at most grocery stores.

How do I apply for SNAP in Alabama?

You can apply online through MyDHR, by mail, or in person at a local county DHR office.

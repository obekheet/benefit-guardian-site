North Dakota Children s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

The Children s Health Insurance Program (CHIP) provides low-cost health coverage to eligible North Dakota children in families that earn too much to qualify for Medicaid but still need affordable coverage. CHIP is administered by North Dakota Health and Human Services and helps families afford care like checkups, immunizations, and more.

What CHIP covers

North Dakota CHIP typically helps cover a broad set of services for children, often including checkups, immunizations, doctor and hospital visits, prescriptions, and dental and vision care. Specific covered services depend on the child s eligibility, so North Dakota Health and Human Services is the best source for details.

Who may qualify

Eligibility for CHIP in North Dakota is based on household income and family size, with income limits that are higher than for many adult Medicaid categories. Many working families qualify. Because limits change, eligibility varies and should be confirmed with the agency.

How to apply

You can apply for North Dakota CHIP online, by phone, by mail, or in person through North Dakota Health and Human Services. The same application is generally used for Medicaid and CHIP, and the state determines which program a child qualifies for based on household details.

Official source: ND Health and Human Services — CHIP

Idaho TAFI (Temporary Assistance for Families)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Temporary Assistance for Families in Idaho (TAFI) is the states TANF cash assistance program. It provides temporary cash benefits for eligible low-income families and households to help pay for food, clothing, shelter, and other essentials.

What is TAFI?

TAFI provides monthly cash payments to help eligible families with children meet basic needs while they work toward self-sufficiency. The benefit amount varies based on family size, income, and circumstances.

Who is eligible?

TAFI generally serves low-income families with children who meet income and resource limits and Idaho residency requirements. Participants are usually expected to take part in work or job-readiness activities. The exact benefit varies by household.

How to apply

You can apply through the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare online, by phone, or in person. You may need to provide documents and complete an interview as part of the process.

Official source

Verify details and apply on the official state page: Idaho Department of Health and Welfare - About TAFI.

Frequently asked questions

How much cash does TAFI provide?

The monthly amount varies depending on your family size, income, and situation. The state determines your benefit when you apply.

Is there a time limit on TAFI?

TANF programs like TAFI generally have time limits and work requirements. Details vary, so confirm with the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare.

Can I get TAFI and SNAP at the same time?

Yes. Many families receive both cash assistance and food assistance. You can apply for them together through the same agency.

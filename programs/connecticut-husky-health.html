Connecticut HUSKY Health (Medicaid)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

HUSKY Health is Connecticut's Medicaid program, providing comprehensive health coverage to eligible children, parents, adults, pregnant individuals, and people with disabilities. It is administered by the Connecticut Department of Social Services.

What HUSKY Health covers

HUSKY Health covers a broad range of services including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, and preventive care. Coverage groups include HUSKY A, C, and D, which together make up Connecticut Medicaid.

Who may qualify

Eligibility is based on income, household size, and category such as children, parents, pregnant individuals, adults, or people who are aged, blind, or disabled. Many residents qualify at no cost.

How to apply

You can apply online through Access Health CT or the Department of Social Services, by phone, or in person. There is no enrollment fee, and you can apply any time of year.

Official source

For full eligibility details and to apply, visit the official Connecticut HUSKY Health page: Connecticut HUSKY Health.

Frequently asked questions

Is HUSKY Health the same as Medicaid?

Yes. HUSKY A, C, and D are Connecticut's Medicaid program. HUSKY B is the separate Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP).

How much does HUSKY Health cost?

Cost varies by program and income. Many HUSKY Health enrollees pay nothing, while some pay low premiums or copays depending on their coverage group.

Can I apply year-round?

Yes. Unlike some marketplace plans, you can apply for HUSKY Health at any time of the year.

California Earned Income Tax Credit (CalEITC)

Last updated June 12, 2026

The California Earned Income Tax Credit (CalEITC) is a refundable state tax credit that puts money back in the pockets of low-income workers and families. For tax year 2025, it is worth up to $3,756 for those earning $32,900 or less, and it can be claimed on top of the federal EITC.

How much can you get?

For the 2025 tax year, the CalEITC is worth up to $3,756. The exact credit depends on your earned income and how many qualifying children you have. Because it is refundable, you can receive it as a refund even if you owe no state income tax.

Who is eligible?

You may qualify if you are a California resident who earned $32,900 or less during the tax year and have a valid Social Security Number or ITIN. Workers both with and without qualifying children can be eligible, and there is no minimum age for many filers with children. The credit is designed for working individuals and families with modest incomes.

How to claim the credit

To claim the CalEITC you must file a California state income tax return and complete Form FTB 3514. If you file electronically, your tax software walks you through the questions. You can generally claim the credit for up to four prior tax years by filing or amending those returns. Official details and the calculator are available from the California Franchise Tax Board at ftb.ca.gov.

Frequently asked questions

How much is the CalEITC worth in 2025?

For tax year 2025, the California Earned Income Tax Credit is worth up to $3,756 for a qualifying working family or individual. The exact amount depends on your earned income and number of qualifying children.

Who qualifies for the California Earned Income Tax Credit?

You may qualify if you are a California resident who worked and earned income of $32,900 or less in the tax year, have a valid SSN or ITIN, and meet the filing requirements. Both workers with and without qualifying children can be eligible.

How do I claim the CalEITC?

You claim the credit by filing a California state income tax return and completing Form FTB 3514. If you e-file, your tax software will guide you through it. You can also claim it for up to four prior tax years by amending past returns.

Can I get CalEITC and the federal EITC at the same time?

Yes. The CalEITC is separate from and in addition to the federal Earned Income Tax Credit, so many workers qualify for both in the same year.

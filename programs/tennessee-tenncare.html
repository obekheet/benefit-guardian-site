TennCare (Tennessee Medicaid)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

TennCare is the Tennessee Medicaid program, administered by the Division of TennCare. It provides health coverage to qualifying low-income residents, including children, pregnant women, parents and caretakers, older adults, and people with disabilities.

What is TennCare?

TennCare is the state of Tennessee Medicaid program. It delivers health care services largely through managed care organizations and covers a broad range of services such as doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, and preventive care. Eligibility and benefits are determined by the Division of TennCare.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility depends on your category, household income, and household size. Coverage pathways exist for children, pregnant women, parents and caretaker relatives, older adults, and people with disabilities. Tennessee has not adopted Medicaid expansion for all low-income adults, so eligibility is category-based. Applying is the best way to learn what you qualify for.

How to apply

You can apply for TennCare through TennCare Connect online, by phone, by mail, or in person. After you apply, you may be asked to verify income, identity, and other details before a coverage decision is made.

Official source

For official, up-to-date information, visit Division of TennCare (tn.gov).

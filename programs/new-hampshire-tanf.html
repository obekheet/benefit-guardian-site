New Hampshire TANF / FANF (Financial Assistance to Needy Families)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

New Hampshire's Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF), delivered through the Financial Assistance to Needy Families (FANF) program, provides temporary cash assistance to eligible low-income families with children. The benefit amount varies based on family size and income.

What FANF / TANF provides

FANF provides monthly cash assistance to help families with children cover basic needs such as housing, utilities, and other essentials. It is intended as temporary support while families work toward stability, and amounts vary by household.

Who is eligible

Eligibility is based on income, family size, and having dependent children in the home. New Hampshire residents who meet income limits and program requirements, which may include work or training participation, may qualify.

How to apply in New Hampshire

Apply through the New Hampshire Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), which administers FANF. Applications can be submitted online, by mail, or in person at a local DHHS District Office.

Frequently asked questions

What is FANF in New Hampshire?

FANF (Financial Assistance to Needy Families) is how New Hampshire delivers its TANF cash assistance program for low-income families with children.

How much cash assistance will I receive?

The amount varies by family size and income. There is no single fixed amount, so check the official DHHS page for current details.

Where do I apply for TANF in New Hampshire?

Apply through NH DHHS online, by mail, or at a local District Office. The official TANF/FANF page has current application links.

North Dakota TANF (Cash Assistance)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) provides temporary cash assistance and support services to eligible low-income families with children in North Dakota. The program is administered by North Dakota Health and Human Services and is designed to help families meet basic needs while working toward self-sufficiency.

What TANF provides

TANF offers monthly cash assistance to help families cover basic needs, along with work and training supports through programs such as JOBS. The exact cash amount depends on household size, income, and other factors, so the benefit varies from family to family and is best confirmed with the agency.

Who may qualify

TANF in North Dakota generally serves low-income families with dependent children. Eligibility considers income, resources, household size, and work-related requirements. Because rules and limits change over time, eligibility varies and should be confirmed directly with North Dakota Health and Human Services.

How to apply

You can apply for North Dakota TANF online, by mail, or in person through North Dakota Health and Human Services. The process typically includes verifying income and household details and may involve participating in work or training activities as a condition of ongoing assistance.

Official source: ND Health and Human Services — TANF

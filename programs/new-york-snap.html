New York SNAP

Last updated: June 18, 2026

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) helps eligible low-income New York households buy groceries each month. In New York, SNAP is administered by the New York State Office of Temporary and Disability Assistance (OTDA) through your local department of social services. Benefits are delivered on a New York State EBT card.

What is SNAP?

SNAP provides monthly food benefits loaded onto a New York State EBT card, which works like a debit card at any store that accepts SNAP, and for approved online grocery orders. Benefits can be used for foods like fruits and vegetables, meat, poultry and fish, dairy, and bread and cereal. They cannot be used for tobacco, alcohol, vitamins, medicines, or non-food items.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources. Working families, older adults, people with disabilities, and others with limited income may qualify. Most adults ages 16 to 59 must follow SNAP work rules. Households where everyone is age 60 or older, or has a disability, may qualify under simplified rules.

How to apply

You can apply for SNAP in several ways. After applying, you will complete an interview and provide documents to verify income and household details.

Apply online

Create an account and apply at myBenefits.ny.gov, where you can also check your case and upload documents.

Apply by phone

Call 1-800-342-3009 (OTDA Hotline) for help applying and to request free language assistance.

Apply by mail or in person

You can also submit an application by mail or at a local department of social services office.

Official source

This guide is based on information from the official program source: New York OTDA — SNAP. Income limits and benefit amounts can change; always confirm current figures with OTDA or your local social services district.

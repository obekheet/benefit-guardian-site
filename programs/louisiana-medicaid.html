Louisiana Medicaid

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Louisiana Medicaid provides health coverage to eligible low-income residents, including children, pregnant women, families, seniors, and people with disabilities. The program is overseen by the Louisiana Department of Health (LDH).

What does Louisiana Medicaid cover?

Louisiana Medicaid covers a broad range of medical services, which can include doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, preventive and behavioral health care, and long-term services and supports for those who qualify. Coverage is generally delivered through managed care plans.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility depends on factors such as income, household size, age, disability status, and pregnancy. Many low-income children and families qualify, and seniors and people with disabilities may qualify under additional pathways. Income limits vary by category.

How to apply

You can apply for Louisiana Medicaid online, by phone, by mail, or in person. The Louisiana Department of Health website provides the application and a list of documents you may need, such as proof of income, identity, and residency.

Frequently asked questions

How do I apply for Louisiana Medicaid?

Apply online, by phone, by mail, or in person. The state reviews your information to determine eligibility.

What does Louisiana Medicaid cost?

Most members pay little or nothing for covered services. Costs vary by program and circumstances; check the official LDH site for current details.

Are children covered by Louisiana Medicaid?

Yes. Children may qualify for Medicaid, and those in families with somewhat higher incomes may qualify for LaCHIP, Louisiana's Children's Health Insurance Program.

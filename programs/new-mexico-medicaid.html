New Mexico Medicaid (Turquoise Care)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

New Mexico Medicaid provides free or low-cost health coverage to eligible residents, including many children, pregnant women, older adults, and people with disabilities. The state delivers most Medicaid benefits through Turquoise Care, its managed care program administered by the New Mexico Health Care Authority.

What Turquoise Care covers

Turquoise Care is New Mexico s Medicaid managed care program. It connects enrolled members to a health plan that coordinates services such as doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, and long-term care. Covered services and any costs depend on your eligibility category, so the agency is the best source for specifics.

Who may qualify

Medicaid eligibility in New Mexico is based on factors such as income, household size, age, disability, and pregnancy. Many low-income adults qualify through Medicaid expansion, and children may qualify through Medicaid or the related CHIP coverage. Because limits change, eligibility varies and should be confirmed with the Health Care Authority.

How to apply

You can apply for New Mexico Medicaid online, by phone, by mail, or in person through the Health Care Authority. After applying, you provide documents that verify income and household details, and if approved you select or are assigned a Turquoise Care health plan.

Official source: NM Health Care Authority — Turquoise Care

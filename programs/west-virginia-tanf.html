WV WORKS (West Virginia TANF)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

WV WORKS is West Virginia's version of Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF). It provides temporary monthly cash assistance and employment support to low-income families with children, administered by the West Virginia Bureau for Family Assistance.

Who is eligible

The program serves low-income families with dependent children who meet income and resource limits. Most adults are expected to take part in employment and training activities. The state reviews your household size, income, and circumstances when you apply.

What you can get

Eligible families receive a monthly cash payment to help cover basic needs, along with employment and support services that help parents move toward self-sufficiency. The cash amount varies based on family size and income.

How to apply

You can apply online, by mail, or in person through the West Virginia Bureau for Family Assistance. After applying, you typically complete an interview and develop a personal responsibility plan as part of the program.

Official source: WV Bureau for Family Assistance

Frequently asked questions

How much cash assistance will I receive?

The amount varies depending on your family size, income, and other factors. The state calculates your monthly payment when your application is approved.

Is there a time limit?

Yes. TANF cash assistance has time limits, though certain extensions may apply in specific situations. The state explains the limits that apply to your case.

Do I have to work to qualify?

Most adults must participate in employment and training activities as part of their personal responsibility plan, with some exemptions based on individual circumstances.

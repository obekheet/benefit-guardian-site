Hawaii Med-QUEST (Medicaid)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Med-QUEST is Hawaii's Medicaid program, providing free or low-cost health coverage to eligible low-income residents, families, children, pregnant individuals, and people with disabilities. It is administered by the Hawaii Department of Human Services, Med-QUEST Division.

What Med-QUEST covers

Med-QUEST covers comprehensive services including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, maternity care, and preventive services. Most members receive care through managed care health plans.

Who may qualify

Eligibility is based on income, household size, and category, including children, parents, pregnant individuals, adults, and people who are aged, blind, or disabled. Many residents qualify at no cost.

How to apply

You can apply online, by mail, or in person through the Med-QUEST Division. Applications are accepted year-round, and there is no enrollment fee.

Official source

For full eligibility details and to apply, visit the official Hawaii Med-QUEST page: Hawaii Med-QUEST.

Frequently asked questions

Is Med-QUEST the same as Medicaid?

Yes. Med-QUEST is the name of Hawaii's Medicaid program, which provides health coverage to eligible residents.

How much does Med-QUEST cost?

For most eligible members Med-QUEST is free or very low cost. Specific costs depend on your eligibility category.

Can I apply at any time?

Yes. There is no limited enrollment period for Med-QUEST; you can apply any time of the year.

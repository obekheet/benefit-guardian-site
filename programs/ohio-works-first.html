Ohio Works First (Cash Assistance)

Last updated: June 13, 2026

Ohio Works First (OWF) is the financial assistance portion of Ohio's Temporary Assistance to Needy Families (TANF) program. It provides time-limited cash benefits to help low-income families with children meet their basic needs.

What is Ohio Works First?

Ohio Works First provides cash benefits to needy families for up to 36 months. It is designed to help families stabilize while working toward self-sufficiency, and participation often includes employment and training activities through the broader Ohio Works program.

Who is eligible?

OWF generally serves low-income families with dependent children (and pregnant individuals). Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources, and is determined by your county Department of Job and Family Services. Because the program is needs-based, your county caseworker will review your specific situation.

How to apply

You can apply online through the Ohio Benefits self-service portal, in person at your county Department of Job and Family Services, or by submitting the Request for Cash, Food, and Medical Assistance (JFS 07200). Most ODJFS programs are administered by your local county agency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can I receive Ohio Works First?

Ohio Works First provides cash benefits to eligible families for up to 36 months.

Is Ohio Works First the same as TANF?

Yes. Ohio Works First is the cash assistance portion of Ohio's federal Temporary Assistance to Needy Families (TANF) program.

Where do I apply?

Through the Ohio Benefits portal online or in person at your county Department of Job and Family Services.

Kentucky Medicaid: Eligibility & How to Apply

Last updated: June 2026

Kentucky Medicaid provides free or low-cost health coverage to eligible residents through the Kentucky Department for Medicaid Services (DMS). This guide explains who qualifies, what is covered, and how to apply using official Kentucky resources.

What is Medicaid in Kentucky?

Medicaid is a joint federal and state health coverage program administered in Kentucky by the Kentucky Department for Medicaid Services (DMS). It serves children, pregnant women, parents and caretakers, adults, seniors, and people with disabilities who meet income and other requirements.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is determined by income, household size, and category. Most applicants under 65 are assessed using MAGI (Modified Adjusted Gross Income) compared to the Federal Poverty Level. Children, pregnant women, and certain adults often qualify at higher income levels than other groups. Income limits vary by group and are updated each year, so confirm current limits with the official source below.

What does Medicaid cover?

  • Doctor visits and specialist care
  • Hospital and emergency services
  • Prescription drugs
  • Laboratory and X-ray services
  • Maternity and newborn care
  • Preventive and wellness services
  • Behavioral health and substance use treatment

Exact covered benefits vary by plan and eligibility group. Review your plan materials or the official source for details.

How to apply

  • Online: Through the Kentucky state benefits portal or HealthCare.gov.
  • By phone: Call 1-855-446-1245.
  • By mail or in person: At a local Kentucky office.

Have proof of income, identity, residency, and household size ready before you apply.

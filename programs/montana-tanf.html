Montana TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Montana TANF provides temporary cash assistance to help eligible families with children meet basic needs while working toward self-sufficiency. It is administered by the Montana Department of Public Health and Human Services (DPHHS).

What is Montana TANF?

TANF stands for Temporary Assistance for Needy Families. In Montana, it offers monthly cash assistance to low-income families with dependent children, along with employment and support services to help families build a path to self-sufficiency. Benefit amounts vary by family size and income.

Who may be eligible?

Eligibility is based on household income, family size, and the presence of dependent children. Families must meet income and resource limits, and most adults participate in work or training activities. The exact cash benefit varies depending on your circumstances.

How to apply for TANF in Montana

You can apply online through apply.mt.gov, by mail, or in person at a local Office of Public Assistance. DPHHS reviews applications, verifies eligibility, and explains any work or training requirements that apply to your household.

Official source: Montana DPHHS - TANF

Frequently asked questions

How much cash assistance does Montana TANF provide?

The amount varies based on your family size, income, and circumstances. DPHHS determines your monthly benefit after reviewing your application.

Are there work requirements for TANF?

Most adults receiving TANF participate in work or training activities. DPHHS explains the specific requirements that apply to your household.

How do I apply for Montana TANF?

Apply online at apply.mt.gov, by mail, or in person at an Office of Public Assistance. DPHHS handles applications.

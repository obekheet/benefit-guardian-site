California CalFresh (SNAP)

Last updated: June 18, 2026

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) helps eligible low-income California households buy groceries each month. In California, SNAP is known as CalFresh and is administered by the California Department of Social Services (CDSS) through county offices. Benefits are delivered on the Golden State Advantage EBT card.

What is CalFresh?

CalFresh provides monthly food benefits loaded onto a Golden State Advantage EBT card, which works like a debit card at any store that accepts SNAP, and for approved online grocery orders. Benefits can be used for foods like fruits and vegetables, meat, poultry and fish, dairy, and bread and cereal. They cannot be used for tobacco, alcohol, vitamins, medicines, or non-food items.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources. Working families, older adults, people with disabilities, and others with limited income may qualify. Most adults ages 16 to 59 must follow SNAP work rules. Households where everyone is age 60 or older, or has a disability, may qualify under simplified rules.

How to apply

You can apply for CalFresh in several ways. After applying, you will complete an interview and provide documents to verify income and household details.

Apply online

Create an account and apply at BenefitsCal.com, where you can also check your case and upload documents.

Apply by phone

Call 2-1-1 or your county office for help applying and to request free language assistance.

Apply by mail or in person

You can also submit an application by mail or at a local CDSS office.

Official source

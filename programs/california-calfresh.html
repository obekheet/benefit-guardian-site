CalFresh: California’s Food Assistance Program (SNAP)

Last updated June 12, 2026

CalFresh is California’s name for the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP). It provides monthly food benefits on an EBT card to help low-income individuals and families afford nutritious food. It is the largest food program in California, state-supervised and county-operated.

How much can you receive?

The amount of CalFresh benefits a household receives varies based on household size, countable income, and monthly expenses such as housing and utilities. Because the benefit is calculated individually for each household, there is no single fixed amount; benefits are issued monthly on an EBT card that can be used at grocery stores and farmers markets that accept EBT.

Who is eligible?

CalFresh is available to many low-income California households. Eligibility depends primarily on household size and income, along with certain other factors. Recipients of some other assistance programs may also qualify. The program is federally mandated and operated locally by each county.

How to apply

You can apply for CalFresh through your county social services office or online. For official program details, eligibility information, and application links, visit the California Department of Social Services at cdss.ca.gov, or call the CalFresh Benefits Helpline at 1-877-847-3663.

Frequently asked questions

How much will I get from CalFresh?

There is no fixed amount. CalFresh benefits depend on your household size, countable income, and certain monthly expenses such as housing and utilities, so the amount is calculated for each household individually.

Is CalFresh the same as SNAP?

Yes. CalFresh is California’s name for the federal SNAP program. It provides the same kind of monthly food benefits, issued on an EBT card.

Where can I use CalFresh benefits?

CalFresh benefits are loaded onto an EBT card each month and can be used to buy eligible food at grocery stores and farmers markets that accept EBT.

How do I apply for CalFresh?

You can apply through your county social services office or online, or call the CalFresh Benefits Helpline at 1-877-847-3663 for help getting started.

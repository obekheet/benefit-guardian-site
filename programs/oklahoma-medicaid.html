Oklahoma SoonerCare (Medicaid)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

SoonerCare is Oklahomas Medicaid program, administered by the Oklahoma Health Care Authority. It provides health coverage to eligible children, pregnant women, adults, seniors, and people with disabilities. What you qualify for and what is covered varies by eligibility category.

Who is eligible

Eligibility for SoonerCare depends on income, household size, age, and category such as children, pregnant women, adults, seniors, or people with disabilities. Income limits differ by group, so the Oklahoma Health Care Authority determines your eligibility when you apply.

What is covered

SoonerCare covers a range of medical services that can include doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, preventive care, and more depending on your eligibility category. Specific covered benefits and any cost-sharing vary by program and member.

How to apply

You can apply for SoonerCare online through the Oklahoma Health Care Authority enrollment portal, by phone, or with help from a local office. You will provide information about income, household, and citizenship or immigration status to determine eligibility.

Official source: Oklahoma Health Care Authority (OHCA) SoonerCare

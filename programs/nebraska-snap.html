Nebraska SNAP (Food Assistance)

Last updated: June 2026

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) in Nebraska helps people with low income buy food. In Nebraska, the program is administered by Nebraska DHHS. This guide explains who qualifies, the maximum monthly benefit amounts, and how to apply.

What is SNAP in Nebraska?

SNAP provides monthly benefits on an EBT card that can be used to buy groceries at most stores and many farmers markets. Benefit amounts depend on household size, income, and certain expenses.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based mainly on household income and size. Most households must have a gross monthly income at or below 130% of the federal poverty level. Work requirements may apply to some adults. Check with Nebraska DHHS for the rules that apply to your situation.

How to apply

  • Online: Nebraska DHHS online application
  • By phone: 1-800-383-4278
  • In person / by mail: Contact your local Nebraska DHHS office.

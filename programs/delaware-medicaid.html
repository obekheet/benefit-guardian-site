Delaware Medicaid

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Delaware Medicaid provides free or low-cost health coverage to eligible low-income families, children, pregnant individuals, seniors, and people with disabilities. It is administered by the Delaware Division of Medicaid and Medical Assistance.

What Delaware Medicaid covers

Medicaid covers a comprehensive set of services including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, maternity care, and preventive services. Most enrollees receive care through managed care plans.

Who may qualify

Eligibility is based on income, household size, and category, including children, parents, pregnant individuals, adults, and people who are aged, blind, or disabled. Many residents qualify at no cost.

How to apply

You can apply online through Delaware ASSIST, by mail, or in person at a Division of Social Services office. Applications are accepted year-round.

Official source

For full eligibility details and to apply, visit the official Delaware DMMA Medicaid page: Delaware DMMA Medicaid.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Delaware Medicaid cost?

For most eligible enrollees Medicaid is free, with little or no out-of-pocket cost. Coverage and any small fees depend on your eligibility category.

Can I apply at any time?

Yes. There is no limited enrollment period for Medicaid; you can apply any time of the year.

Does Delaware Medicaid use managed care?

Yes. Most Delaware Medicaid members receive services through a managed care organization that coordinates their care.

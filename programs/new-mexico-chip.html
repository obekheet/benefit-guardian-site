New Mexico Children s Medicaid (CHIP)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

New Mexico provides health coverage for eligible children through Medicaid and the Children s Health Insurance Program (CHIP). These programs help families afford doctor visits, immunizations, dental and vision care, and more. Children s coverage is delivered through the state s Medicaid program, Turquoise Care, administered by the New Mexico Health Care Authority.

What children s coverage includes

Children enrolled in New Mexico Medicaid and CHIP can receive a broad set of services, often including checkups, immunizations, doctor and hospital visits, prescriptions, and dental and vision care. Specific covered services depend on the child s eligibility category, so the agency is the best source for details.

Who may qualify

Eligibility for children s coverage in New Mexico is based on household income and family size, with income limits that are higher than for many adult categories. Many working families qualify. Because limits change, eligibility varies and should be confirmed with the Health Care Authority.

How to apply

You can apply for children s Medicaid and CHIP in New Mexico online, by phone, by mail, or in person through the Health Care Authority. The same application is used for Medicaid, and the state determines whether a child qualifies for Medicaid or CHIP based on household details.

Official source: NM Health Care Authority — Turquoise Care

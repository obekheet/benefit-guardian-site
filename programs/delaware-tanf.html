Delaware TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) is Delaware's main cash assistance program, providing monthly support to low-income families with children. It is administered by the Delaware Division of Social Services in partnership with other state agencies.

What TANF provides

TANF provides monthly cash assistance to help eligible families with children cover basic needs. The program is paired with employment and training services to help families move toward self-sufficiency.

Who may qualify

Eligibility is based on income, household size, and the presence of dependent children. Most adult recipients are expected to participate in work or training activities.

How to apply

You can apply online through Delaware ASSIST, by mail, or in person at a Division of Social Services office. Bring proof of identity, income, household composition, and expenses.

Official source

For full eligibility details and to apply, visit the official Delaware DSS TANF page: Delaware DSS TANF.

Frequently asked questions

How much cash assistance does Delaware TANF provide?

The monthly amount varies based on family size, income, and other factors. The Division of Social Services calculates your benefit when you apply.

Do I have to work to receive TANF?

Most adults must take part in employment or training activities as a condition of receiving TANF, with support provided through partner agencies.

Is there a time limit on TANF?

Yes. TANF is time-limited cash assistance, consistent with federal rules, though specifics depend on individual circumstances.

Wisconsin Medicaid for Children (CHIP)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Wisconsin Medicaid and BadgerCare Plus provide health coverage for eligible children, including the state's Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) coverage. These programs are administered by the Wisconsin Department of Health Services.

What coverage is available for children?

Wisconsin offers comprehensive health coverage for eligible children through Medicaid and BadgerCare Plus, including doctor visits, immunizations, prescriptions, dental, vision, and hospital care. CHIP coverage helps children in families with incomes above full Medicaid limits get affordable coverage.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility for children's coverage is based on the child's age, household income, and household size. Children in lower-income households may qualify for full coverage, while those in slightly higher-income families may qualify through CHIP. Applying is the best way to learn what your child qualifies for.

How to apply

You can apply online through the ACCESS portal, by mail, by phone, or in person at a local agency. After applying, you may need to verify income and household details.

Official source

For official, up-to-date information, visit Wisconsin DHS Medicaid Programs for Children (dhs.wisconsin.gov).

Frequently Asked Questions

How does CHIP work in Wisconsin?

CHIP coverage in Wisconsin is provided through Medicaid and BadgerCare Plus for eligible children, especially those in families above full Medicaid income limits.

Who administers children's Medicaid in Wisconsin?

The Wisconsin Department of Health Services administers Medicaid and BadgerCare Plus, including children's coverage.

Where do I apply for children's coverage?

You can apply through the ACCESS portal online, by mail, by phone, or in person at a local agency.

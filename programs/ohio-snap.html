Ohio SNAP (Food Assistance Program)

Last updated: June 13, 2026

Ohio's Food Assistance Program is the state's version of the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP). It helps Ohioans with lower incomes afford nutritious groceries through the Ohio Direction Card.

What is Ohio Food Assistance?

SNAP helps people with low incomes buy healthy food. If you qualify, you receive an Ohio Direction Card, which works like a debit card you can use at approved grocery stores and farmers markets. The program is administered by the Ohio Department of Job and Family Services (ODJFS) through your local county agency.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on household size, income, and resources. SNAP is generally available to households with limited income and assets. Because exact income limits are updated each year and vary by household size, Ohio encourages you to apply and let your county caseworker determine eligibility. You can begin an application through the Ohio Benefits self-service portal.

How to apply

You can apply online through Ohio Benefits, in person at your county Department of Job and Family Services, or by submitting the Request for Cash, Food, and Medical Assistance (JFS 07200). Most ODJFS programs are administered locally, so your county agency handles your case.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Ohio Direction Card?

It is the electronic benefit card Ohio issues to people approved for food assistance. You swipe it like a debit card at approved stores to pay for eligible groceries.

Where can I use Ohio SNAP benefits?

At approved grocery stores, retailers, and participating farmers markets across Ohio.

How do I check if I qualify?

Apply through Ohio Benefits or your county agency, and a caseworker will review your household size, income, and resources to determine eligibility.

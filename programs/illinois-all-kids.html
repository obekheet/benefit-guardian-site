Illinois All Kids (Children's Health Insurance)

Last updated: June 13, 2026

All Kids is Illinois' health insurance program for children, providing comprehensive coverage to eligible kids through age 18 regardless of immigration status. Together with the FamilyCare program for parents and pregnant individuals, it is administered by the Illinois Department of Healthcare and Family Services (HFS).

What does it cover?

All Kids covers a full range of health benefits, including doctor visits, dental and vision care, immunizations, hospital care, prescriptions, and more. The program has several plan levels — All Kids Assist, All Kids Share, and All Kids Premium Levels 1 and 2 — with cost-sharing that increases at higher income tiers. Children in the lowest-income plan (Assist) have no co-payments or premiums.

Who is eligible?

Children through age 18 who live in Illinois are eligible, regardless of citizenship or immigration status. Income determines the specific plan: children in families with countable income within 147% of the federal poverty level qualify for All Kids Assist with no premiums or co-payments, while higher income tiers (up to 318% FPL) qualify for plans with modest premiums and co-payments. Parents and caretaker relatives may qualify for FamilyCare up to 138% FPL, and pregnant individuals up to 213% FPL through Moms and Babies.

How to apply

Families can apply online through the All Kids and ABE websites in English or Spanish, or download a paper application to apply by mail. All Kids Application Agents (AKAAs) provide free help with applications. For more information, visit the Illinois HFS All Kids resources.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for All Kids in Illinois?

Children through age 18 who live in Illinois, regardless of immigration status. Income determines the specific plan, from All Kids Assist (up to 147% FPL with no cost) to higher tiers with modest premiums.

Is there a cost for All Kids?

Children in All Kids Assist have no premiums or co-payments. Higher income tiers may have modest monthly premiums and co-payments, capped per family per year.

How do I apply for All Kids?

Apply online through the All Kids or ABE website in English or Spanish, by mail, or with free help from an All Kids Application Agent.

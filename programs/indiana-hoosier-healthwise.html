Hoosier Healthwise (Indiana CHIP)

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Hoosier Healthwise is Indiana's health care program for children up to age 19 and pregnant individuals. Indiana's Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) falls under Hoosier Healthwise, providing coverage at little or no cost depending on family income.

What is Hoosier Healthwise?

Hoosier Healthwise covers medical care such as doctor visits, prescription medicine, mental health care, dental care, hospitalizations, and surgeries. It has two benefit packages: Package A is full coverage with no cost-sharing for children and pregnant individuals, and Package C covers children enrolled in CHIP with a small monthly premium and some copays based on income.

Who is eligible?

Hoosier Healthwise serves children up to age 19 and pregnant individuals. CHIP, which falls under Hoosier Healthwise, covers children whose families have slightly higher incomes than full Medicaid allows. Eligibility is based on household income and size. Applying is the best way to learn what you qualify for.

How to apply

You can apply online through the FSSA Benefits Portal, by phone, by mail, or in person at a local Division of Family Resources office. When you enroll, you select a managed care health plan. After applying, you may need to verify income and household details.

Official source

For official, up-to-date information, visit Indiana Medicaid Hoosier Healthwise (in.gov).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CHIP part of Hoosier Healthwise?

Yes. Indiana's Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP) falls under the Hoosier Healthwise program.

Who does Hoosier Healthwise cover?

It covers children up to age 19 and pregnant individuals, with CHIP serving children in families with slightly higher incomes.

Where do I apply for Hoosier Healthwise?

You can apply through the FSSA Benefits Portal online, by phone, by mail, or in person at a local DFR office.

New Mexico TANF (Cash Assistance)

Last updated: June 15, 2026

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) provides temporary cash assistance and support services to eligible low-income families with children in New Mexico. The program is administered by the New Mexico Health Care Authority and is designed to help families meet basic needs while working toward self-sufficiency.

What TANF provides

TANF offers monthly cash assistance to help families cover basic needs, along with services that support employment and stability. The exact cash amount depends on household size, income, and other factors, so the benefit varies from family to family and is best confirmed with the agency.

Who may qualify

TANF in New Mexico generally serves low-income families with dependent children. Eligibility considers income, resources, household size, and work-related requirements. Because rules and limits change over time, eligibility varies and should be confirmed directly with the Health Care Authority.

How to apply

You can apply for New Mexico TANF online, by mail, or in person through the Health Care Authority. The process typically includes verifying income and household details and may involve participating in work or training activities as a condition of ongoing assistance.

Official source: NM Health Care Authority — TANF

Arizona Medicaid (AHCCCS)

Last updated: June 13, 2026

AHCCCS (the Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System) is Arizona's Medicaid program. It offers medical coverage and resources to Arizona residents who meet certain income and other requirements.

What does AHCCCS cover?

AHCCCS provides comprehensive medical coverage, including doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, behavioral health, maternity care, and preventive services. The program serves a wide range of groups and connects eligible members to managed care health plans.

Who is eligible?

AHCCCS offers programs for many groups, including adults, caretaker relatives, children, pregnant women, newborns, individuals age 65 or older, individuals who are blind or have a disability, and working individuals with a disability. Eligibility is based on income and category. Applying is the best way to learn which AHCCCS program fits your situation.

How to apply

You can apply online through Health-e-Arizona Plus, by phone, or in person. If you are not eligible for AHCCCS, you may still qualify for certain behavioral health services through your Regional Behavioral Health Authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does AHCCCS stand for?

AHCCCS stands for the Arizona Health Care Cost Containment System, which is Arizona's Medicaid program.

Who can apply for AHCCCS?

Arizona residents who meet income and other requirements, including adults, children, pregnant women, older adults, and people with disabilities.

Where do I apply for AHCCCS?

Through Health-e-Arizona Plus online or with help by phone or in person.

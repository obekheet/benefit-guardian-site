Idaho Medicaid

Last updated: June 14, 2026

Idaho Medicaid provides free or low-cost healthcare coverage for eligible low-income people and families, including children, pregnant women, the elderly, and people with disabilities. It is administered by the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare.

What does Idaho Medicaid cover?

Medicaid covers a broad range of services such as doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, preventive care, and more. Covered services and any costs can vary depending on the plan and your eligibility category.

Who is eligible?

Eligibility is based on income, household size, and category such as children, pregnant women, parents, seniors, or people with disabilities. Idaho expanded Medicaid coverage to many low-income adults. The exact eligibility limits vary by situation.

How to apply

You can apply online, by phone, or in person through the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare. You may be asked to verify income, residency, and household details before coverage begins.

Official source

Verify details and apply on the official state page: Idaho Department of Health and Welfare - Medicaid & Health.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Idaho Medicaid cost?

For most eligible people coverage is free or very low cost. Any premiums or copays vary by program and income.

Does Idaho Medicaid cover adults?

Yes. Idaho expanded Medicaid, so many low-income adults can qualify based on income in addition to children, seniors, and people with disabilities.

How do I apply for Idaho Medicaid?

Apply online, by phone, or in person through the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare. Processing times vary.

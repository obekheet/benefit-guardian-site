What is a virtual credit card number?

A disposable stand-in for your real card number that keeps your account safer online. Here is how it works.

A virtual credit card number is a temporary, computer-generated number linked to your real credit card account. You use it in place of your actual card number when shopping online, so the merchant never sees your true details. It is one of the simplest ways to add a layer of safety to online spending. Here is the plain-English version.

How a virtual card number works

When your card issuer or a payment app supports them, you can generate a virtual number that charges back to your real account. Purchases still appear on your normal statement and earn your normal rewards.

Because it is a stand-in, if that virtual number is ever stolen in a data breach, your real card number stays protected. You can delete the virtual number without having to replace your physical card.

Why people use them

The main reason is safety. If a website you bought from is hacked, thieves get only a virtual number you can switch off, not your actual card. That can save you the hassle of replacing a card and updating every autopay tied to it.

Some virtual-number tools also let you lock a number to a single merchant or set a spending cap, which is handy for free trials and subscriptions that are hard to cancel.

When a virtual number is most useful

Virtual numbers shine for one-off purchases from unfamiliar online stores, for free trials where you want to control what gets charged, and for any site you do not fully trust with your real details.

They are less suited to situations where you may need to show the physical card, such as picking up a rental car or a will-call ticket tied to the card used to book.

How to get one

Availability depends on your issuer and the apps you use. Many issuers offer virtual numbers through their website or mobile app, and some browsers and payment services provide their own version.

Check your card issuer’s app or website to see whether the feature is offered and how to generate a number. The exact steps and limits vary, so follow your issuer’s instructions.

Common questions

Is a virtual card number the same as my real card?

It is linked to your real account, so charges appear on your normal statement and earn your usual rewards, but the number itself is different. That difference is what protects your real card details online.

Do virtual card numbers cost anything?

Issuers that offer them typically provide the feature at no extra charge, but availability and any terms depend on your specific issuer. Check your card’s app or website to confirm.

When should I not use a virtual card number?

Avoid them when you may need to present the physical card, such as some rental car pickups or will-call ticket collection, since the merchant may need to match the card used to book.

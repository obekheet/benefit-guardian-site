Your credit cards may be handing you free money. Here is how to grab it.

Cash back, statement credits, free subscriptions, travel perks — most people never claim them. Our free tool shows you what is yours in about a minute.

Here is the part nobody tells you: the credit cards already in your wallet may be handing you free money every month. Cash back. Statement credits. Free subscriptions. Travel and shopping protection. Most people never claim it, simply because they do not know it is there. This guide shows you what to look for and how to grab it, free, in about a minute.

What “free money” actually means

It is not a gimmick. A perk is free money when you would have spent the money anyway and the card pays you back, or when the card covers a cost you would otherwise pay out of pocket. Cash back on groceries you were buying. A credit that wipes out a streaming bill. A refund when a new gadget breaks. You are not chasing anything new — you are claiming value you already earned.

The catch is that issuers rarely make these perks obvious. They are buried in benefit guides most people never open. That is exactly the gap this tool was built to close.

The free money most people leave behind

A few of the big ones: unredeemed cash back and points just sitting in your account; statement credits for dining, travel, or rideshare that reset every month or year; free subscriptions bundled with the card; and protections like purchase coverage, extended warranty, and trip insurance that quietly replace money you would have lost.

Any one of these can be worth more than a card’s annual fee. Stacked together, they can add up to hundreds of dollars a year you are currently walking past.

Why it is easy to miss

Two reasons. First, the perks are spread across long, dense terms that assume you already speak “credit card.” Second, many credits only count if you remember to use them before they reset — so a benefit you do not know about expires without you ever touching it.

You should not need a finance degree to get what you already pay for. The whole point of Benefit Guardian is to translate the fine print into plain English and hand you a simple list.
